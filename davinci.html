Mona Lisa

  • Created: 1503-1506
  • Medium: Oil on poplar panel

  • Description: Mona Lisa is perhaps the most famous painting in the world, renowned for the subject's enigmatic smile and the use of sfumato, a technique of blending colors and tones. Painted during the Renaissance, this portrait of Lisa Gherardini, the wife of a wealthy Florentine merchant, captures a moment of subtle emotional expression, blending realism with idealism. The mysterious quality of her smile has fascinated viewers for centuries.

Lady with an Ermine

  • Created: 1489-1490
  • Medium: Oil on canvas

  • Description: Lady with an Ermine is a portrait of Cecilia Gallerani, the mistress of Ludovico Sforza, Duke of Milan. Da Vinci's exquisite attention to detail is evident in the soft rendering of her face and the delicate fur of the ermine. The relaxed yet dignified pose of the subject, along with her graceful expression, captures a moment of intimate yet sophisticated portrayal, demonstrating Da Vinci's skill in human psychology and naturalism.

Christ Salvator Mundi

  • Created: 1500
  • Medium: Oil on panel

  • Description: Christ Salvator Mundi shows Jesus Christ holding a crystal orb in one hand and raising the other in blessing. The painting's careful depiction of Christ's serene and divine presence, along with its intricate details and soft use of light, are hallmarks of Da Vinci's mastery. The painting gained international attention in recent years due to its high auction price, affirming its status as one of Da Vinci's most significant works.

Adoration of the Magi

  • Created: 1481
  • Medium: Oil on wood panel

  • Description: This unfinished work portrays the Three Wise Men (Magi) presenting gifts to the infant Jesus. The composition features a dramatic interaction between the figures, with Da Vinci's characteristic use of light and shadow to create depth. The piece is notable for its complex arrangement and the dynamic poses of the figures, showcasing Da Vinci's understanding of human anatomy and emotion.

The Annunciation

  • Created: 1472-1475
  • Medium: Tempera on panel

  • Description: The Annunciation depicts the angel Gabriel announcing to the Virgin Mary that she will conceive the Son of God. The painting is a masterful example of Renaissance perspective and realism, with Da Vinci's careful attention to light, space, and texture. The serene yet dramatic depiction captures the divine moment, showcasing Da Vinci's early skill in portraying religious scenes with a naturalistic touch.

The last Supper

  • Created: 1495-1498
  • Medium: Tempera on gesso, pitch, and mastic

  • Description: The Last Supper is one of the most iconic religious paintings, depicting the moment when Jesus Christ announces that one of his disciples will betray him. The composition masterfully captures the emotional reaction of the apostles, with each figure's expression and body language conveying a unique response to the news. The use of perspective and the placement of the figures in space creates a sense of depth and drama, highlighting Da Vinci's innovation in both technique and storytelling.
