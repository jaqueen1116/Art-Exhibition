The Starry Night

  • Created: 1889
  • Medium: Oil on canvas

  • Description: One of Van Gogh's most iconic works, The Starry Night depicts a swirling night sky over a small village, showcasing vibrant blues and yellows. The painting was created during his time in a mental asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence. It represents a sense of longing, with the night sky conveying both beauty and turbulence.

Vase with Fifteen Sunflowers

  • Created: 1888
  • Medium: Oil on canvas

  • Description: This vibrant piece is part of a series of sunflower paintings Van Gogh created while living in Arles. Vase with Fifteen Sunflowers is known for its bold use of yellow tones, symbolizing warmth and life. The flowers are arranged in a way that evokes both beauty and fragility, demonstrating Van Gogh's fascination with nature.

The Night Café

  • Created: 1888
  • Medium: Oil on canvas

  • Description: The Night Café depicts a dimly lit, chaotic café scene, using strong reds and greens. Van Gogh wanted to evoke a sense of unease and discomfort in this painting, choosing these colors to create an intense, unsettling atmosphere. It is believed to symbolize the darker side of human nature, as Van Gogh sought to capture the feeling of isolation and despair.

Starry Night Over the Rhone

  • Created: 1888
  • Medium: Oil on canvas

  • Description: This painting captures the view of the Rhône River at night, with stars reflecting on the water. Van Gogh contrasts the tranquil nature of the river with the brilliance of the stars in the sky. The painting also features the reflections of gaslight from the nearby town, illustrating Van Gogh's ability to combine natural beauty with urban life.

Irises

  • Created: 1889
  • Medium: Oil on canvas

  • Description: Painted while Van Gogh was at the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum, Irises showcases a vibrant display of blue and purple flowers set against a bright, earthy background. This piece represents Van Gogh's deep connection to nature, and the irises symbolize hope and resilience, conveying his psychological and emotional state at the time.

Bedroom in Arles

  • Created: 1888
  • Medium: Oil on canvas

  • Description: This famous interior scene shows Van Gogh's bedroom in the Yellow House in Arles, with simple furniture and vibrant colors. The painting reflects his desire for tranquility and stability after a tumultuous period. The distorted perspective of the room suggests the artist's emotional unrest, as he painted it during his stay in Arles, where he hoped to create a space of peace and creativity.

Almond Blossoms

  • Created: 1890
  • Medium: Oil on canvas

  • Description: Almond Blossoms was painted as a gift for Van Gogh's brother, Theo, to celebrate the birth of his son. The painting captures the delicate beauty of blooming almond trees, symbolizing new beginnings, renewal, and hope. The soft blue background contrasts with the vibrant white and pink flowers, creating a serene and optimistic image.
